## What's New in v3.0

### Complete IHCC Cohort Inclusion

The IHCC explicitly "recognizes the value of smaller cohorts representing underrepresented or unique groups." v3.0 includes **all** IHCC member cohorts, not just those with ≥100K participants:

| Criterion | v1.0/v2.0 | v3.0 |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Cohort source | Arbitrary selection | IHCC Global Cohort Atlas |
| Size threshold | ≥100K only | All IHCC members |
| LMIC inclusion | 7 cohorts | 24 cohorts (35% of IHCC) |
| Total cohorts | 27 | 89 |

### Key IHCC Statistics (Connolly et al. 2025)

- **89 cohorts** from **43 countries**
- **~34 million** unique research participants
- **35%** from LMIC locations (N=24)
- **65%** hold genomic data (N=45)
- **14%** linked to EMR (N=10)

## IHCC Membership Criteria

From Connolly et al. (2025):

> "IHCC membership criteria prioritize large population cohorts, capability for longitudinal health follow-up, broad participant selection, biological sample collection, and a commitment to data-sharing. **It also recognizes the value of smaller cohorts representing underrepresented or unique groups.**"

### Full Criteria

1. **Population focus**: Broad participant selection (not disease-specific)
2. **Longitudinal**: Capability for health follow-up
3. **Biosamples**: Biological sample collection
4. **Data sharing**: Commitment to share data/metadata
5. **Size**: Preferably ≥100K, but smaller cohorts accepted for:
   - Low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)
   - Low-resource settings (LRS)
   - Underrepresented populations
   - Unique demographic groups

## Key Metrics

### 1. Equity Alignment Score (EAS)

Per-cohort measure (0–100) of research alignment with global disease burden:

```
EAS = 100 - mean(Gap Scores for diseases with ≥1 publication)
```

### 2. Research Gap Score

Per-disease measure (0–100) of research deficit relative to burden:

| Category | Gap Score | Interpretation |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Critical | > 70 | High burden, minimal research |
| High | 50–70 | Significant underrepresentation |
| Moderate | 30–50 | Some attention needed |
| Low | < 30 | Adequate coverage |

### 3. HIC:LMIC Ratio

```
Ratio = Publications(HIC cohorts) / Publications(LMIC + LIC cohorts)
```

### 4. Geographic Coverage Index

```
GCI = (Regions with cohort publications) / (Regions with disease burden)
```
