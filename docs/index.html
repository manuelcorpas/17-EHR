About HEIM-Biobank

The Equity Alignment Score (0-100) measures how well a biobank's research portfolio matches global disease burden. Higher scores indicate better alignment between research output and health needs.

The Research Gap Score (0-100) quantifies the mismatch between a disease's global burden and research attention. Categories: Critical (>70) High (50-70) Moderate (30-50) Low (<30)

Methodology: Corpas et al. (2025), Annual Review of Biomedical Data Science

About HEIM-Biobank

Methodology, data sources, and how to interpret this dashboard

üéØ What is HEIM-Biobank?

HEIM-Biobank (Health Equity Informative Metrics for Biobanks) is a tool that measures how well biobank research aligns with global health priorities. It answers a critical question: Are biobanks studying the diseases that cause the most suffering worldwide?

Many biobanks are located in high-income countries and tend to focus on diseases prevalent in those regions. Meanwhile, conditions like malaria, tuberculosis, and diarrheal diseases‚Äîwhich cause enormous suffering in low- and middle-income countries‚Äîreceive relatively little attention. HEIM-Biobank quantifies these gaps to guide more equitable research priorities.

üìä Data Sources

Global Burden of Disease (GBD) 2021

Disease burden data comes from the Global Burden of Disease Study 2021, published by the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME). This is the world's most comprehensive effort to measure health loss from diseases, injuries, and risk factors.

  • DALYs (Disability-Adjusted Life Years): The primary measure of disease burden. One DALY = one year of healthy life lost, either through premature death or living with disability.
  • 179 disease causes are analyzed, organized into categories (e.g., Cardiovascular, Infectious, Neurological).
  • Global estimates are used to represent worldwide health priorities.

PubMed / MEDLINE

Publication data is retrieved from PubMed, the largest database of biomedical literature. Publications are:

  • Retrieved using biobank-specific search queries
  • Mapped to GBD disease categories using MeSH (Medical Subject Headings) terms
  • Filtered to include only peer-reviewed articles (preprints excluded)

Biobank Registry

The dashboard currently includes 27 major biobanks worldwide, classified by:

  • WHO Region: AFR (Africa), AMR (Americas), EMR (Eastern Mediterranean), EUR (Europe), SEAR (South-East Asia), WPR (Western Pacific)
  • Income Status: High-Income Countries (HIC) vs Low/Middle-Income Countries (LMIC)

üìê Key Metrics Explained

Equity Alignment Score (EAS)

A score from 0-100 measuring how well a biobank's research output matches global disease burden. Higher is better.

EAS = 100 - (0.4 √ó Gap_Severity + 0.3 √ó Burden_Miss + 0.3 √ó Capacity_Penalty)
  • Strong (‚â•70): Research well-aligned with global health priorities
  • Moderate (50-69): Some alignment, room for improvement
  • Weak (30-49): Significant gaps in priority disease coverage
  • Poor (<30): Research focus misaligned with global burden

Research Gap Score

A score from 0-100 for each disease measuring the mismatch between disease burden and research attention. Higher = bigger gap (worse).

  • Critical (>70): Severely under-researched relative to burden
  • High (50-70): Substantially under-researched
  • Moderate (30-50): Moderately under-researched
  • Low (<30): Research roughly proportional to burden

Research Opportunity Score (ROS)

For each biobank, this represents the cumulative disease burden of conditions with minimal research coverage (‚â§2 publications). A higher ROS indicates more untapped potential for impactful research.

Equity Ratio

Compares research intensity between high-income and low/middle-income country biobanks:

Equity Ratio = (Pubs_HIC / DALYs_HIC) / (Pubs_LMIC / DALYs_LMIC)

A ratio of 1.0 would indicate equal research intensity. Ratios >100 indicate severe imbalance favoring HIC research.

üß≠ How to Use This Dashboard

Overview Tab

Start here for a global summary: total biobanks analyzed, publications mapped, and critical research gaps across all diseases.

Biobanks Tab

Explore individual biobank performance. Filter by region or equity category. Click column headers to sort.

Diseases Tab

See which diseases are under-researched. Filter by category or gap severity. High-burden diseases with few publications represent urgent priorities.

Matrix Tab

Visualize the intersection of biobanks and diseases. Darker cells = more publications. Gaps (empty cells) reveal opportunities.

Trends Tab

Track publication growth over time by biobank. Identifies which resources are expanding most rapidly.

Themes Tab

Understand research themes based on MeSH term clustering. See which methodological approaches (GWAS, imaging, etc.) dominate.

Compare Tab

Side-by-side comparison of any two biobanks across multiple dimensions.

Equity Tab

Deep dive into HIC vs LMIC disparities. See Global South priority diseases and regional publication distribution.

üìñ Glossary

Biobank A repository that stores biological samples (blood, tissue, DNA) linked to health records for research.
DALYs Disability-Adjusted Life Years. Combines years of life lost (mortality) and years lived with disability (morbidity) into a single measure.
GBD Global Burden of Disease Study. A comprehensive assessment of mortality and disability from major diseases worldwide.
HIC High-Income Countries. Nations with high gross national income per capita (e.g., USA, UK, most of Europe).
LMIC Low- and Middle-Income Countries. Nations with lower gross national income (e.g., most of Africa, South Asia, Latin America).
MeSH Medical Subject Headings. A controlled vocabulary used to index biomedical literature in PubMed.
WHO Region Geographic regions defined by the World Health Organization: AFR, AMR, EMR, EUR, SEAR, WPR.
Global South Term referring to countries in Africa, Latin America, Asia, and Oceania that are generally lower-income and bear disproportionate disease burden.
